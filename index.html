Gold Investment Guide

Gold Rush is a free Malaysian gold & silver price tracker by FR Ventures. We combine live international spot prices with the current USD/MYR rate, estimated dealer premiums, real technical analysis and zakat tools — all in one app. Below are answers to common questions about gold investment in Malaysia.

How is the gold price in Malaysia calculated?
Malaysian gold prices start from the international spot price, quoted in US dollars per troy ounce (31.1035g). This is converted to Ringgit using the live USD/MYR exchange rate, then divided into a per-gram price. Dealers add a premium of roughly 3–18% on top of spot to cover minting, distribution and profit — that is why retail prices are always higher than the spot price shown in our Compare tab.
What is the difference between 999, 916 and 750 gold?
The number refers to purity in parts per thousand. 999 (24K) is 99.9% pure gold, used for investment bars and coins. 916 (22K) is 91.6% pure and is the standard for Malaysian jewellery because it is more durable. 750 (18K) is 75% gold mixed with other metals. Investment buyers usually choose 999 because you pay less for craftsmanship and it is easier to resell at a fair price.
What are mayam, serial and tahil in Malaysian gold trading?
Besides grams, Malaysian gold is still traded in traditional units. One mayam is 3.71g — the everyday unit in east-coast gold shops, especially Kelantan and Terengganu, where jewellery and dowry gold are quoted per mayam. One serial is 2.72g, an older Kelantanese measure still used by local goldsmiths. One tahil (tael) is 37.8g, the goldsmith and bullion trade unit — wholesale price boards often quote ringgit per tahil. Tap the unit pill beside the live price on our Live tab to switch the whole app between g, mayam, serial and tahil instantly.
Should I buy physical or digital gold?
Physical gold (bars, coins, jewellery) is yours to hold with no counterparty risk, but you must store it safely and spreads are wider. Digital gold (bank gold accounts and apps) lets you start from as little as RM10 with instant buy/sell and tighter spreads, but you rely on the provider’s credibility and most accounts are unallocated. Many Malaysians mix both: digital for monthly saving, physical for long-term wealth. Use the Physical/Digital filter in our Compare tab to see which is which.
What is the spread and why does it matter?
The spread is the gap between a dealer’s selling price and their buy-back price, shown as a percentage on each card in our Compare tab. If you buy at RM600/g and the dealer buys back at RM540/g, the spread is 10% — gold must rise 10% before you break even. A lower spread means you recover your cost faster, so compare spreads, not just the sticker price.
When is a good time to buy gold?
Nobody can time the market perfectly, but two approaches work well. Dollar-cost averaging — buying a fixed amount every month — smooths out price swings. Buying dips: our Signal indicator flags when the price falls below its recent average with an oversold RSI, historically a better entry point. Long-term holders worry less about timing: gold in ringgit has risen substantially over the past decade as a hedge against inflation and currency weakness.
How is zakat on gold calculated in Malaysia?
Zakat is due when your gold holdings exceed the nisab (85g of pure gold) and have been held for one full year (haul). The rate is 2.5% of current market value. Gold jewellery that is regularly worn is exempt up to the uruf limit (commonly 800–850g depending on state). Use our Zakat tab — it tracks your nisab progress and haul from your portfolio automatically, and links directly to all 14 official state zakat bodies for online payment.
Is gold a good investment in Malaysia?
Gold is primarily a store of value, not an income asset — it pays no dividends but protects against inflation and ringgit depreciation. Because gold trades in US dollars, a weaker ringgit lifts the local price even when the world price is flat. Many advisers suggest holding 5–15% of a portfolio in gold for diversification. This is general information, not financial advice.
How do I store physical gold safely?
Common options: a home safe (fast access, but arrange insurance), a bank safe deposit box (roughly RM100–400 per year), or dealer vault storage offered by companies like Public Gold. Keep purchase receipts and certificates — they matter greatly at resale. Never store large amounts unsecured at home.
How do I sell gold at the best price?
Compare buy-back prices first — the Cheapest sort and Best Sell badge in our Compare tab show who pays the most today. Selling back to the dealer you bought from is usually smoothest; bring the original certificate and receipt. Jewellery loses the workmanship fee (upah) at resale, so bars and coins retain value better. Avoid panic-selling during dips; our Signal can help you spot overbought peaks instead.
What makes gold prices move?
The big drivers are US interest rates (higher rates make gold less attractive versus bonds), inflation data, the strength of the US dollar, central-bank buying, and geopolitical crises that trigger safe-haven demand. For Malaysians the USD/MYR rate adds a second layer — a weaker ringgit raises local prices. Our chart marks major market events so you can see these effects across history.
